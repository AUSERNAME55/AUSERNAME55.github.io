Displaying the Pocket Money of Peachblossoms Students Using a Histogram

What are histograms? Histograms are a way of showing statistical datas by using class intervals and frequency. Class intervals are the histograms's width and the frequency is the histograms's height. The frequency is related to how many values are in that class interval. In this diagram, we show the pocket money that students bring to school each day by showing the data as a histogram.
